The motivation for folded-bias decomposition is architectural.
In the baseline flow, CSA-based accumulation is followed by an explicit comparator add with $-Th$, whose carry-out
gives the final decision bit.
Folded-bias instead injects $K$ directly in CSA columns and obtains the same decision bit without a separate
comparator stage.
A small example at $n=13$ illustrates the effect: FA count decreases by $\Delta\mathrm{FA}=4$ (26.7\%),
and raw $k$-LUT structural size decreases by $\Delta\mathrm{raw\text{-}}k\mathrm{\text{-}LUT}=12$ (26.1\%) versus baseline.
